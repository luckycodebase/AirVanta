Air Quality Health Tips

When to Wear a Mask

Wear N95 masks when AQI exceeds 150, especially during outdoor activities.

Improve Indoor Air

Use HEPA filters, air purifiers, and keep indoor plants to maintain good air quality.

Exercise Safely

Avoid strenuous outdoor exercise when AQI is above 100. Indoor workouts are recommended.

Ventilation

Keep windows closed during high pollution days. Use air filters regularly.

Plant Trees

Plant native trees to naturally reduce pollution in your neighborhood.

Reduce Emissions

Use public transport, walk, or cycle when possible. Good for your health and the environment.

Environmental Awareness

Climate Action

Air pollution is a major environmental concern. By monitoring AQI and making sustainable choices, you contribute to a healthier planet.

Why Air Quality Matters

Poor air quality affects respiratory health, mental health, and longevity. Real-time monitoring helps you make informed decisions.

What You Can Do

Reduce car usage, support renewable energy, plant trees, use eco-friendly products, and advocate for clean air policies.

About Our AQI Calculation:

  • Current Data: Real-time AQI from WAQI monitoring stations
  • Training Data: City-wise AQI history stored locally from WAQI updates
  • Fallback: If history is limited, WAQI-based synthetic history is used
  • Model: Linear regression with weekly seasonal adjustment for 30-day forecast
  • Auto Learning: Forecast improves as more daily WAQI entries are collected
